Legislators

State legislators are elected to serve as lawmakers and members of a legislative body. Most legislators possess a bachelor's degree, although no formal education is required. They do have to meet their state's age and residency requirements. The breadth of a legislator's duties varies by state, and for most of these professionals, legislating is a part-time job. While there are no official education requirements, age and residency requirements for becoming a legislator differ by state.
